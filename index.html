FROM THE FOUNDERS

A note before we begin.

For over a decade, our industry — AV, IT, security, automation, building systems — has rewarded us. It has also shown us the same gap every year: bright young people who want to enter this trade, with nowhere to learn it.

We hire by word of mouth. We train on the job. We all complain about the same shortage — and none of us has done much about it. Until now.

We have decided to do something about it.

— Sawan & Sameer, Co-Founders, MCBEE Skills Foundation
Sawan Nichani Founder / CEO
Sameer Nichani Founder / COO
WHAT WE STAND FOR

We are building India's most comprehensive industry-led training ecosystem for AV, IT, security, automation, and building systems — making world-class skills accessible to underprivileged youth, and giving today's installers the recognition their craft deserves. Within the next decade, every Indian integrator should be able to hire from a recognised national pool of trained, certified professionals.

THE MOMENT WE'RE IN

Two forces are meeting. We can either let them collide — or build a bridge.

FORCE ONE

AI is reshaping work.

Cognitive work is automating fast. What holds value? The physical, hands-on, real-world skills — installing systems, terminating cables, commissioning at 11pm. That's our trade.

FORCE TWO

India's youth are arriving.

62% of India is working-age. Median age: 29. Millions entering the workforce each year without serious technical pathways. Without intervention, our talent shortage gets worse, not better.

The team

Two tracks. One mission.

A self-sustaining model where the industry funds the training of its own future workforce.

TRACK ONE

Free for the youth who need it most.

Underprivileged young people, 18+, given a fully sponsored pathway into the trade. Structured training, hands-on lab work, and industry placement — at no cost to them.

Funded through CSR, donations, and Track Two surplus.

TRACK TWO

Certification for existing installers.

Working installers across AV, IT, security, and automation get formal, recognised certification. Modular, paid programmes designed around real-world schedules.

Surplus from Track Two underwrites Track One.

Built to be recognised — by industry, then by government.

01

Industry-led standards

Senior practitioners review and sign off every curriculum, assessment, and trainer qualification. Industry sets the bar.

02

Real-world assessment

Students design, build, and commission actual systems — not multiple-choice exams. The certificate proves they can do the work.

03

Government recognition

Every course is designed against the National Skills Qualifications Framework from day one. Formal NSQF alignment is the 2028 target.

Real dignity

"We are not opening a door for the sake of it. We are opening it onto a real career, with real income, in a real industry."

A shared industry challenge — and a real opening.

No training pathway

No recognised institute, no industry curriculum, no national qualification for an integration technician — across AV, IT, security, automation, or BMS.

We compete for the same small pool

When a project lands, we look for trained hands. Finding none, we poach from each other. None of us, alone, was going to fix this.

Demand is multiplying

Smart buildings, hospitality AV, retail security, corporate IT — the work is growing faster than the industry's ability to staff it.
